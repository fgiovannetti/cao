Abstract

The Critical Apparatus Ontology (CAO) is an OWL 2 DL ontology for representing the apparatus of textual variants and philological arguments that accompanies a digital critical edition.

The CAO model provides a set of classes and properties which are useful for representing a critical apparatus, from the creation of a new apparatus entry, to the description of the readings and their relationships, to the formalization of the process of scholarly interpretation.

CAO is based on the TEI guidelines and incorporates classes and relationships from other conceptual models: the Web Annotation Data Model is used for describing scholarly annotations and for linking such annotations to the critical text; specific properties of the PROV-O ontology are used to declare provenance of interpretations and to describe certain types of relationship among textual variants; FRBRoo is used to describe witnesses according to different layers of interpretation.

The development of CAO aims at being a first step towards the publication of data-centric digital critical editions to facilitate the federation of cultural resources on the web.
