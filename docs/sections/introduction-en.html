Introduction back to ToC

The Critical Apparatus Ontology (CAO) is an OWL 2 DL ontology for representing the apparatus of textual variants and philological arguments that accompanies a digital critical edition. In line with the philosophy of the Text Encoding Initiative (TEI), CAO does not commit to any specific school of textual criticism.

The CAO model provides a set of classes and properties which are useful for representing a critical apparatus, from the creation of a new apparatus entry, to the description of the readings and their relationships, to the formalization of the process of scholarly interpretation.

Consistently with best practice, which encourages reuse of existing ontologies, CAO is based on the TEI guidelines and incorporates classes and relationships from other conceptual models to solve specific issues: the Web Annotation Data Model is used for describing scholarly annotations and for linking such annotations to the critical text (which is normally contained in a separate XML document); specific properties of the PROV-O ontology are used to declare provenance of interpretations and to describe certain types of relationship among textual variants; FRBRoo is used to describe witnesses according to different layers of interpretation.

The development of CAO aims at being a first step towards the publication of data-centric digital critical editions to facilitate the federation of cultural resources on the web.
